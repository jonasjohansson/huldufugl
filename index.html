About

Award-winning Icelandic creative studio creating immersive, theatrical experiences with technology and spaces since 2015. Huldufugl means "hidden bird" in Icelandic.

Huldufugl are innovators, collaborators and trailblazers. Often each project breaks new ground worldwide, whether creating one of the first live theatre shows in VR, collaborating on musical history, exploring storytelling through circus theatre and motion capture, or engaging citizens in public art experiences.

Origin Story

Founded when Nanna Gunnars and Owen Hindley moved from London to Iceland. Owen worried about finding work, and Nanna said: "Well let's form a theatre company together then, so you'll always have something to keep you busy."

Fallax

Iceland's first narrative VR film. A 23-minute wordless journey through Icelandic mythology, combining motion-captured circus performers with procedurally generated landscapes.

At a Glance

TypeVR Film (6DoF)
Duration23 minutes
LanguageWordless
CountriesIceland / UK
Year2025
DirectorOwen Hindley
ProducerNanna Gunnars
CompaniesHuldufugl + GunHil + Hikapee
StatusFestival touring; platform release late 2026

Synopsis

An escaped convict flees into the Icelandic highlands with accomplices. When they vanish overnight, a glowing light draws the fugitive into an otherworldly portal — a liminal space where time and nature move differently. The film transforms a heist thriller into an ethereal encounter with Icelandic mythology, with motion-captured aerial circus artists embodying elemental spirits in a wordless, musical journey.

Visual Style

Developed using procedural modeling in Houdini. The Icelandic landscape is algorithmically "shredded" into thousands of individually colored polygons — maintaining sharp VR edges without textures, enabling precise color control, pre-baked static lighting, and vertex shader effects for wind-movement simulation in trees, water, and moss.

"It's a unique challenge to produce animated work for VR — where almost anything is possible in terms of angles, lighting, sound and set design." — Owen Hindley

Origin

Emerged when Huldufugl's stage production with Hikapee (in development since 2018) paused due to COVID-19. The team channeled the collaboration into a VR short using the same circus performers, captured via motion capture at 101 Outdoor Arts in Newbury, UK.

All Projects

Kassinn / A Box In The Desert 2017-2019

Interactive VR Theatre — 1 audience member — 20 min — 300+ performances

One audience member enters a VR world where they're trapped in a box in the desert. A voice pushes them to escape while a live actor tries to convince them to stay. Dual Oculus Rift with multiplayer networking.

4 Awards: A MAZE Berlin (Most Amazing Game), Raindance (Best Interactive Narrative), San Diego Fringe (Outstanding US Premiere), Stockholm Fringe (Innovation in Performance)

"We think of it as theater. But we took it to a games festival and got an award as the best game." — Nanna Gunnars

Parallel People / Hlidstaett Folk 2020-2022

Interactive VR Theatre for 5 — Oculus Quest

Theatre experience about trust, communication, and questioning information sources. Five audience members are trapped in transparent boxes in a hostile landscape. Sold out at National Theatre of Iceland. Nominated at Raindance Immersive 2022.

The Hidden People 2018-2022

90-min aerial circus theatre — Collaboration with Hikapee

Spectacular show combining circus arts, Icelandic folklore, and creative technology. Large-scale projection mapping on sets and performers. TouchDesigner + Houdini + Unity. Explores the controversial hydroelectric dam construction through huldufolk mythology.

Askur Yggdrasils Maze 2016

370 sqm outdoor public art installation — 3,000+ visitors

Immersive labyrinth built from shipping pallets in central Reykjavik for Culture Night. 16+ unique spaces by 24 contributing artists. Inspired by Nordic mythology and the Poetic Edda.

ABBA Voyage Launch 2021

Live event production — global livestream

Local production for ABBA's worldwide album launch livestream at Sky Lagoon, Reykjavik.

Of Monsters And Men / Fever Dream Day 2019

Street performance happening

Eight masked dancers through downtown Reykjavik with hidden synchronized speakers. Custom internet radio station.

Strategy & Roadmap

The Pitch

Award-winning Icelandic studio creating immersive experiences at the intersection of VR, circus theatre, and Icelandic folklore.

Unique Strengths

  1. Genuine theatre background — not a tech company playing at art

  5. Strong award track at both games (A MAZE) and film (Clermont-Ferrand) festivals
