## 1. Venice Immersive

### Project Description — Fallax (300 words)

Fallax is Iceland's first narrative VR film: a 23-minute, wordless journey through Icelandic mythology that fuses motion-captured circus performance with procedurally generated landscapes.

An escaped convict flees into the Icelandic highlands. When their accomplices vanish overnight, a glowing light draws the fugitive through an otherworldly portal into a liminal space where time and nature obey different rules. What begins as a heist thriller dissolves into an ethereal encounter with elemental spirits — embodied by aerial circus artists whose movements were captured at 101 Outdoor Arts in the UK by motion capture specialists Target3D.

The film's visual language is built entirely in Houdini. Iceland's terrain is algorithmically deconstructed into thousands of individually coloured polygons — no textures, only geometry and light. This approach delivers razor-sharp edges in VR while enabling precise colour control, pre-baked lighting, and vertex shader effects that simulate wind moving through trees, water, and moss. The result is a landscape that feels simultaneously real and impossible.

Fallax is wordless by design. Its story is told through movement, music (composed by Iris Thorarins), and spatial sound design. The 6DoF format allows audiences to inhabit the world rather than simply observe it — leaning into details, turning to follow spirits, feeling the scale of the highlands around them.

The film premiered at Art*VR Prague and won the Immersive Award at Imagine Film Festival Amsterdam. It has since screened at Animateka Ljubljana, QLD XR Brisbane, Clermont-Ferrand, NewImages Paris, and Laval Virtual. A fulldome adaptation is currently in development.

Fallax was produced by Huldufugl (Iceland) with GunHil and Hikapee (UK), supported by Creative Europe MEDIA, the Icelandic Film Centre, and Arts Council England. It represents a decade of collaboration between theatre makers and technologists, rooted in the belief that virtual reality is a performing art.

### Artist Statement (150 words)

Huldufugl makes work at the intersection of theatre, technology, and landscape. We believe virtual reality is not a screen — it is a stage. Our practice begins with the body: performers, breath, weight, risk. We then extend that physicality into digital space, using procedural tools to build worlds that respond to the logic of movement rather than photorealism.

Iceland shapes everything we make. Its geology, mythology, and light inform our visual language and storytelling. We are drawn to liminal spaces — thresholds between the familiar and the strange — because that is where audiences become participants.

Our work has been recognised at festivals including Imagine, A MAZE, Raindance, and Clermont-Ferrand. But what drives us is the moment an audience member removes a headset and looks at the real world differently. That shift in perception is the art.

### Technical Description (200 words)

Fallax is built in Unity (URP) for Meta Quest headsets, delivering a 6DoF VR experience at 72fps. The film runs as a standalone application with no external hardware requirements.

All environments were modelled procedurally in Houdini. Iceland's terrain — mountains, valleys, coastlines, moss fields — is algorithmically decomposed into thousands of individual polygons, each assigned a specific colour value. This texture-free approach produces sharp edges native to VR rendering, eliminates texture memory overhead, and enables precise artistic control over colour palettes and atmospheric shifts.

Pre-baked static lighting provides cinematic quality without real-time rendering cost. Vertex shaders drive environmental animation — wind movement in trees, water flow, moss undulation — creating a living world from static geometry.

Character animation uses motion capture data recorded at 101 Outdoor Arts (UK) by Target3D, featuring three aerial circus performers from Hikapee. Capture data was cleaned, retargeted, and applied to stylised character models in Unity. Creative rigging enabled physically impossible movements — extensions of circus vocabulary into digital space.

The spatial audio mix uses Unity's built-in spatialiser with custom attenuation curves. The original score by Iris Thorarins and sound design by Almut Schwacke and Pierre-Marie Blind are mixed for 360-degree spatial playback, anchoring musical and environmental elements to specific positions in the virtual world.

## 5. SAT Montreal / Fulldome Residency (250 words)

We are seeking a residency to develop the fulldome adaptation of Fallax, our award-winning VR film (Immersive Award, Imagine Film Festival; Clermont-Ferrand; NewImages Paris; Laval Virtual).

Fallax is a 23-minute wordless narrative combining motion-captured circus performers with procedurally generated Icelandic landscapes. Its visual language — texture-free geometry, algorithmically coloured polygons, vertex-driven environmental animation — was designed for immersion. The translation to fulldome is a natural next step: expanding a headset experience into a shared, architectural one.

The adaptation is not a simple re-render. Fulldome demands a fundamentally different spatial grammar — the audience is surrounded rather than embodied, collective rather than solitary. We will re-choreograph camera paths, redesign the spatial audio mix for dome speaker arrays, and explore how the procedural landscape can respond to the dome's unique geometry. Key sequences will be re-composed to use the full hemisphere, placing audiences inside volcanic landscapes and among aerial performers at architectural scale.

Our procedural pipeline (Houdini + Unity) is well-suited to dome output. The texture-free polygon aesthetic scales cleanly to high-resolution dome masters without the resolution penalties of texture-based rendering.

SAT's Satosphere and technical expertise make it the ideal environment for this adaptation. We are particularly interested in exploring real-time dome rendering possibilities alongside the pre-rendered version.

Huldufugl has been supported by Creative Europe MEDIA, the Icelandic Film Centre, and Arts Council England. The fulldome adaptation of Fallax is also part of Dome Dreaming (Stockholm, 2026), an initiative by Jonas Johansson exploring immersive dome experiences in the Nordic region.

## 12. Fallax Full Synopsis (150 words)

An escaped convict flees into the Icelandic highlands with two accomplices, seeking refuge in the country's vast uninhabited interior. They make camp, but by morning the others have vanished. Alone, the fugitive notices a strange, glowing light on the horizon.

Drawn toward it, they cross through a portal into a liminal space — an otherworldly version of Iceland where time moves differently and the landscape itself seems alive. Here, elemental spirits appear: ethereal figures who move with the vocabulary of aerial circus, embodied through motion-captured performances by artists from Hikapee.

The spirits guide the fugitive through a series of increasingly surreal environments — volcanic formations, impossible water features, moss fields that breathe — each built from procedurally generated geometry in Houdini. The journey is wordless, carried by an original score by Iris Thorarins and immersive spatial sound design.

What began as an escape becomes a transformation. The fugitive does not return to the world they left.
